# Memory

Jackson is working on a reflective medical case assessment for final year medical school. The case involves a 66-year-old man admitted with a right frontal brain abscess associated with ventriculitis who underwent stereotactic needle aspiration and EVD placement. Patient had a background lung abscess since late last year. Jackson's reflection needs to follow SOLO taxonomy Level 4/5 framework: describe stimulus event with full clinical narrative and personal context, reflect on internal response and assumptions challenged, research and analyse with literature citations (brain abscess pathophysiology, stereotactic aspiration vs excision, lung abscess → brain abscess progression, ring-enhancing lesion differentials, EVD/ventriculitis management), decide on new clinical behaviours and systems-level changes, and act & evaluate with measurable next steps. The strongest reflective thread is continuity of care failure — the lung abscess could have been prevented from seeding to brain if properly managed, representing a key learning point about systems-level infection management and prevention of downstream complications.
