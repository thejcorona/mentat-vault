Lumra’s Most Wanted and Honorable Mentions
From it’s original movie debut in 2023 to the release of the card in 2024, our ol’girl has gone through many different iterations. As more people have started playing the deck some cards have gone from staples, to outcasts, all the way back to staples again. This portion of the primer is dedicated to listing as many of these 101st cards out as possible and listing some of the reasons that most Lumra players have a love/hate relationship with them.

Altar of Dementia : This powerhouse let’s you kill Lumra with her ETB on the stack, mill an additional 10+ cards, and than get that many extra lands on the battlefield. As the list has progressed we've found sac outlets more readily available it has become less necessary. A great reason to add this card is if you want to run the more artifact heavy version of the list alongside cards like Inventors' Fair and Tree of Tales.

Hermit Druid : Hermit Druid was in most versions of Lumra from a very early time until only just recently. Even with the amount of basics that the deck runs it was consistently netting upwards of 10-15+ lands in our graveyard that we could than bring back to the battlefield. In the current meta we've found that it is slightly to slow for the speed that we're trying to go. Card quality has also increased enough that we no longer need 20-25% of our deck in the graveyard to make a win attempt. Staxier versions of this list continue to see a lot of success with this card and while it may not be an auto-include, it more than carries it’s own weight!

Sylvan Library : This card is a mono-green staple that has a high inclusion rate in multi-color wedges as well. The only reason that it’s not ubiquitous in Lumra decks is that it signals a slow start where we are neither turbo-ing out or stax-ing the board. It does however give us an additional safety net whenever we have to mulligan very low. 10/10 card. Can’t wait to cut it in a week only to put it back in after I lose to TnK.

Sowing Mycospawn : Mycospawn is theoretically one of the best land tutors the deck could have. It fetches on cast, enters untapped, and gives us a body for cradle or food chain. Unfortunately, it’s high CMC and inability to be trigger ed using Mirrorpool make this a card that sadly is often the first to go whenever a new card is being tested. It’s a card we love to see, but can’t stop cutting.

Vexing Buable : A high value low cost stax piece that barely effects anything we intend to do during a game. It tends to be cut after it gives a game away and than gets readded after playing against the local RogSi player. Lumra is always walking the tight-rope of trying to protect our wins via stax without paving the way for somebody to win on top of us. Personal experience will highly influence your inclusion or exclusion of this card. Nobody will question you adding nor will they judge you for removing it (except for in SilentViolins who will judge you heavily for cutting it).

Primeval Titan : Currently banned but we make sacrifices and oblations to WotC daily.

Will of the Sultai : A new combo piece that is basically a second version of our commander. It still goes infinite with Mirrorpool but at an even cheaper rate! However it is still a sorcery making it suffer from many of the same problems that cards like Scapeshift and Traverse the Ulvenwald do.
